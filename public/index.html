FRC Team 1884

Griffins

Griffins 1884 is the American School in London’s FRC team, based at One Waverley Place in London. The team identity dates to 2004, with an FRC rookie year of 2006, and the program now spans lower school, middle school, FTC, and FRC pathways inside ASL robotics.

Competition Planning Strategy App Alliance planning, match boards, score models, and event prep. Data Collection Scouting App Live scouting intake, team notes, and event-side collection tools.
ASL describes the program as a progression from Lego robotics and FIRST LEGO League into FTC and FRC, with students learning collaboration, fabrication, programming, electronics, pneumatics, CAD, and game strategy.

Program Snapshot

ASL’s robotics program runs across all school divisions. Lower school students start with engineering and computational thinking; middle school students move into Lego robotics, FIRST LEGO League, Java-based robotics, and FTC; high school students can join the FRC team and work on real-world design, build, and competition problems.

In high school, ASL highlights hands-on work in CAD, web design, fabrication, electronics, pneumatics, programming, outreach, and strategy. The mentor group listed by ASL includes Muktar Ali, Chris Goff, Dr. Karen Bonthrone, Dr. Jan Ostman-Lind, Carlos Alvarez-Santos, Barbara Kim, Mariano Belinky, Tugrul Matras, and alumni mentors.

Sources: ASL Robotics and The Blue Alliance.

Team Identity

The Griffins public profiles tie the team to London, the American School in London, and sponsors including the Gene Haas Foundation. ASL positions robotics as a long-term learning path rather than a single club, with age-appropriate challenges that build toward FRC.

This site now separates the two operational tools used around the team: strategy for match planning and scouting for field data collection.
